<p><p>The agricultural chelates market encompasses various types including EDTA, EDDHA, DTPA, and IDHA, each serving distinct purposes in nutrient availability and soil health. EDTA (Ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid) is widely used for micronutrient delivery, while EDDHA (Ethylenediamine-N,N'-bis(2-hydroxyphenylacetic acid)) enhances iron availability in alkaline soils. DTPA (Diethylenetriaminepentaacetic acid) improves the stability of nutrients in solutions, and IDHA (Isodecyl Hidroxyacetic Acid) offers eco-friendlier options. The "Others" category includes various alternative chelating agents catering to specific agricultural needs.</p></p>
