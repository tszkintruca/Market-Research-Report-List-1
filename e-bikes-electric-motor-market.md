<p><p>The electric bike market features two primary motor types: hub drive and mid-drive. Hub drive motors are located in the wheel and provide direct power, simplifying installation and maintenance while offering versatility for city commuting. In contrast, mid-drive motors are positioned at the bike's crank, delivering a more balanced weight distribution and efficient energy utilization, especially on varied terrains. Each type caters to different rider preferences, influencing performance, handling, and overall riding experience in the growing e-bike market.</p></p>
