<p><p>Wood fireproofing coatings are essential for enhancing the fire resistance of wooden structures. The market is primarily divided into two types: water-based and solvent-based coatings. Water-based coatings utilize water as the primary solvent, offering low volatile organic compounds (VOCs), easy application, and lower environmental impact. In contrast, solvent-based coatings use organic solvents, often providing superior adhesion and durability, making them suitable for harsher environments. Both types aim to protect wood while addressing fire safety regulations and performance requirements.</p></p>
