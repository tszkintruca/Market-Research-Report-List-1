Fe-based superalloys, primarily composed of iron, offer good strength and oxidation resistance, making them suitable for lower-temperature applications. Ni-based superalloys are dominant in high-temperature environments due to their exceptional thermal stability and corrosion resistance, ideal for turbine components. Co-based superalloys provide superior wear resistance and strength at elevated temperatures, making them valuable for specialized aerospace applications. Each type plays a distinct role in engine performance and efficiency.</p></p>
