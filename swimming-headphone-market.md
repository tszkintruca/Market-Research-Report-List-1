<p><p>The swimming headphone market primarily comprises two types: in-ear earbuds and bone conduction headphones. In-ear earbuds fit snugly within the ear canal, offering sound isolation and stability in the water. They are often designed to be waterproof and secure during swimming. Bone conduction headphones, on the other hand, transmit sound through the bones of the skull, allowing wearers to maintain awareness of their surroundings while enjoying music. Both types cater to different user preferences and swimming environments.</p></p>
