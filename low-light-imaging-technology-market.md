<p><p>Low-light imaging technology encompasses various types, primarily Charge Coupled Devices (CCD) and Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor (CMOS) sensors. CCD sensors excel in capturing high-quality images with minimal noise, making them ideal for low-light conditions, commonly used in professional cameras and astronomical applications. Conversely, CMOS sensors are more power-efficient and cost-effective, integrating processing capabilities on the chip, which allows for faster image capture. Both technologies cater to diverse applications in security, medical imaging, and consumer electronics under low-light scenarios.</p></p>
