<p><p>Wet chemical fire suppression systems are designed to combat fires, particularly those involving flammable liquids and cooking oils. They come in two main types: direct release systems and indirect release systems. </p><p>Direct release systems discharge the suppression agent directly onto the fire, providing immediate control. In contrast, indirect release systems release the agent into the space above the cooking area, allowing it to flow down and blanket the flames, ensuring a broader area of coverage for effective fire suppression.</p></p>
